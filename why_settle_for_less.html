Why Settle for Less?

Do you know the trend we made, A fingerprint, hands, and feet displayed? Symbols of us, on my phone it stayed, A keepsake of the love we portrayed.

It’s been there, untouched, since that day, Our last together before I went away. I guarded it like a precious song, But now I see it’s been gone for long.

I asked if yours remained the same, That memory crafted in love’s name. A plan I made with heart so true, A trend, a dream, just us two.

Yesterday, September twenty-seven, I learned it's gone—my heart was broken. What once brought smiles now brings me pain, That core memory washed away in rain.

So, I took mine down to ease the ache, I’m tired of giving while others take. You’d wonder why I feel this way, But it’s not just the trend that’s led me astray.

Where are the letters I wrote with care? Are they hidden, does he still compare? Does he read them, think of me too? Does he love as deep as I always do?

Why settle for less when love should be whole? Why hold back when I’ve given my soul?

Why settle for less?
